What Is Attar? Meaning, History & Uses

Attar is an oil-based, alcohol-free perfume made from natural ingredients. Traditionally crafted using slow distillation, attars are known for their long-lasting fragrance and skin-friendly nature.

India, especially Kannauj, has been producing attars for centuries using traditional techniques that preserve the purity of aroma.

Kannauj: The Perfume Capital of India

Kannauj is globally recognized as the perfume capital of India due to its centuries-old tradition of fragrance manufacturing. The region specializes in natural attars and perfume oils.

Difference Between Attar and Perfume

While perfumes are alcohol-based, attars are oil-based and alcohol-free. This makes attars more suitable for sensitive skin and longer wear.

Understanding this difference helps retailers choose the right product mix for their customers.
