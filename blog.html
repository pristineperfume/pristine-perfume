What is Attar? Meaning, History & Uses

Attar is an oil-based, alcohol-free perfume derived from natural botanical ingredients. Traditionally crafted using slow hydro-distillation, attars are known for their long-lasting fragrance.

India, especially Kannauj, has been the heart of traditional attar making for centuries.

Why Kannauj is Called the Perfume Capital of India

Kannauj has preserved the ancient deg-bhapka distillation process passed down through generations.

Difference Between Attar and Alcohol-Based Perfume

Attars are concentrated oils with no alcohol, making them ideal for sensitive skin and religious use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is attar better than perfume?

Attars last longer, are alcohol-free and suitable for sensitive skin.

Is attar used daily?

Yes, attars are suitable for daily wear due to their natural composition.

Which attar is best from Kannauj?

Rose, jasmine, sandalwood and oud-based attars are highly popular.
